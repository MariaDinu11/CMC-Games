Game Rules

The players place the land tiles turn by turn. This leads to the growth of roads, cities, cloisters and farms, to which the players may deploy their followers in order to earn points. As points can be won during the game as well as at the end, the winner will only emerge during the final scoring.

The player whose turn it is carries out the following actions in the order given:

1. The player must draw one new land tile and place it.

2. The player may draw one follower from his or her supply and deploy it to the tile just placed.

3. If any roads, cities, or cloisters are completed through the placement of the tile, they must be scored now.

After a road, city, or cloister has been completed and scored – and only then – any thieves, knights, or monks involved are returned to their owner. From the next turn onwards, the player can then use them again in whatever role he or she chooses.

The game ends at the end of the turn in which the last land tile is placed. Any roads, cities, and cloisters completed in this round are scored as usual. This is followed by the final scoring.
